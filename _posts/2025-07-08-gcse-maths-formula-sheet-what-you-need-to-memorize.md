<h2>🧮 Essential GCSE Maths Formulas to Memorize 📝</h2>
<p>Here's a list of essential GCSE Maths formulas you should commit to memory:</p>
<p>1. BIDMAS/BODMAS/PEDMAS Rule: An acronym that helps you remember the order of operations in maths problems.</p>
<h2>Brackets/parentheses ( )</h2>
<h3>Indices/Exponents (^)</h3>
<h3>Division (/) and Multiplication (×)  do these operations from left to right</h3>
<h4>Addition (+) and Subtraction ()  do these operations from left to right</h4>
<h4>2. Basic Algebraic Formulas</h4>
<h4>Factoring: `x²  y² = (x + y)(x  y)`</h4>
<h5>Quadratic Formula: `x = (b ± √(b²  4ac)) / (2a)`</h5>
<p>Foil Method: `(a + b)(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd`</p>
<h5>3. Trigonometric Ratios</h5>
<p>Sine (sin), Cosine (cos), and Tangent (tan) for 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90° angles</p>
<h5>Pythagorean Theorem: `a² + b² = c²`</h5>
<h5>4. Geometry Formulas</h5>
<h5>Area of a circle: `πr²`</h5>
<h5>Circumference of a circle: `2πr`</h5>
<h5>Volume of a sphere: `4/3πr³`</h5>
<h5>💡 Memorization Strategies for GCSE Maths Formulas 📝</h5>
<h5>1. Flashcards</h5>
<p>Create flashcards with formulas and their explanations. Review them regularly to reinforce your memory.</p>
<h5>2. Mnemonics</h5>
<p>Use mnemonics to help you remember complex formulas more easily. For example, BIDMAS can be remembered as "Brackets, Indices, Division and Multiplication, Addition and Subtraction."</p>
<h5>3. Practice Problems</h5>
<p>Solve practice problems that involve the formulas you're trying to memorize. This will help you apply the formulas in realworld situations.</p>
