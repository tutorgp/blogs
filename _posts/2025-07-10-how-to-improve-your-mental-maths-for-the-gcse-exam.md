<p>Mental maths is an essential skill for GCSE Maths success. It not only saves time during exams, but it also demonstrates a deep understanding of mathematical concepts. Here are some tips to help you boost your mental maths skills:</p>
<p>1. Practice regularly: Just like any other skill, regular practice is key to mastering mental maths. Dedicate a specific time each day to honing your mental maths abilities.</p>
<p>2. Break down problems: Large problems can seem intimidating. Break them down into smaller, manageable parts to make them less daunting and easier to solve.</p>
<p>3. Use reallife examples: Connecting maths problems to realworld situations can make them more relatable and easier to understand. For example, calculating the cost of a sale or the number of apples needed for a recipe.</p>

<p>2. Mnemonics: Create memory aids to help remember difficult facts or formulas. For example, using the phrase "My Very Elegant Mother Just Served Us Nine Pizzas" (MEVJUSNP) to remember the order of operations (PEMDAS).</p>
<p>3. Active recall: Regularly test your knowledge by recalling information without looking at your notes. This helps reinforce memory and improves understanding.</p>
